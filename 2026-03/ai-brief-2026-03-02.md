## 📌 One to Watch

The self-reinforcing financial architecture revealed by OpenAI's $110 billion round — where cloud providers and chipmakers invest capital that flows straight back to them as purchase commitments — is crystallizing into a defining structural pattern for AI's next phase. Watch for this flywheel dynamic to intensify: as frontier model companies require ever-larger compute investments, their biggest backers (Amazon, Microsoft, Nvidia, Google) effectively become both funders and primary beneficiaries, creating a closed loop that raises serious questions about competition, market concentration, and whether independent AI challengers can realistically emerge. Regulators in the EU and increasingly in the U.S. are likely to scrutinize these circular investment structures closely in 2026, especially as OpenAI's valuation now rivals the GDP of mid-sized nations.
