## 📌 One to Watch

The Anthropic-Pentagon fallout is more than a one-company story — it's the opening salvo in what may become a defining fault line in AI governance: whether AI safety constraints are a feature or a liability when national security is involved. With OpenAI rapidly signing a DOD deal to replace Anthropic, commercial AI firms now face a stark strategic choice about how much to align with government clients and on whose terms. As AI models are increasingly tested in military simulations with alarming escalatory tendencies, and as the Trump administration signals it will reward companies willing to strip away ethical guardrails for defense use, expect more AI firms to quietly recalibrate their safety postures — or face being frozen out of the largest government contract market in the world. The long-term question: will 'safety-first' AI development become a competitive disadvantage in the U.S. national security market?
